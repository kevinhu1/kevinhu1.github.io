Learn ANYTHING!

MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) (real courses with start/end dates)
Coursera
- Free interactive online courses with real professors and discussion boards. Wider selection of courses but arguably less rigorous than EdX and Udacity.
EdX
- Free interactive online courses with real professors and discussion boards, non-profit, founded by Harvard and MIT. Fewer selection but arguably more rigorous than Coursera.
Udacity
- Work on real projects with their Nanodegree program "by Silicon Valley" (all other courses are self-paced without start/end date). Hefty but worthwhile monthly fee.
Udemy
- Lifetime access to videos and materials by course (some free). Learn ANYTHING, from code to design to yoga.

Open Material/Videos (complete access, but no interactivity)
Gibbon
- Playlists for learning.
Gitbook
- Open source (mostly software) books online as a service. Written with Markdown, published using Git(hub). Less than a year old!
Khan Academy
- Learn anything and everything (from kindergarten to quantum physics!) for free, in form of videos. Non-profit.
lynda.com
- Business, technology, and creative video courses. Monthly fee for access to unlimited courses. Few free courses.
MIT OpenCourseWare
- Open material from past MIT courses.
Stack Social
- Groupon-style bundle video courses
Stanford Online
- Open material from past Stanford courses.
Tuts+
- 19,050+ free step-by-step webpage tutorials. 470+ video courses for a monthly fee.
webcast.berkeley
- Open material from past UC Berkeley courses.
